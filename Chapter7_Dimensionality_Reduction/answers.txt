1. What are the main motivations for reducing a dataset’s
dimensionality? What are the main drawbacks?

Motivations: Remove noise(overfitting), Speed up training,
Drawbacks: Loss of info, Reduced Interpretability

2. What is the curse of dimensionality?

Spcae grows exponentially, Data becomes sparse.
Distance loses meaning.
Need exponentially more data to cover space well.


3. Once a dataset’s dimensionality has been reduced, is it possible to
reverse the operation? If so, how? If not, why?

Depends, but not perfectly. Reverse transforms are approximations.



4. Can PCA be used to reduce the dimensionality of a highly
nonlinear dataset?

No.
Highly non linear data -> curved structure PCA can not capture it well.


5. Suppose you perform PCA on a 1,000-dimensional dataset, setting
the explained variance ratio to 95%. How many dimensions will
the resulting dataset have?

Depends, 
Highly redundant data -> few featuers
Diverese data -> many featuers

6. In what cases would you use regular PCA, incremental PCA,
randomized PCA, or random projection?

regular PCA: Data fits in memory, exact solution
incremental PCA: Dataset is too large to fit in memory, need batches/streaming.
randomized PCA: Dataset is large + high dimensional, Want faster approximate PCA
random projection: Only care about distance preservation (not interpretability).

7. How can you evaluate the performance of a dimensionality
reduction algorithm on your dataset?

Explained Variance(PCA): % of variance retained
Reconstruction error: Project down → reconstruct → compare to original


8. Does it make any sense to chain two different dimensionality
reduction algorithms?

Yes, Speed + Refinement
Noise reduction first then second method captures the structure better.
